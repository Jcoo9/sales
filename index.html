Structural "We have to buy media through our internal trade desk." ›
Why reps lose here
They treat this as a hard no and back off. It's not a no — it's a routing issue. The trade desk is a buyer, not a blocker. Most reps don't know how to sell through it.
What to say
Word for word →"Totally understand — that's pretty common at your scale. Help me understand how your trade desk evaluates new inventory sources. Is it purely on price and scale, or do they consider things like audience exclusivity and brand safety certifications? Because that changes how I'd position this for them."
Next moves
→ Ask for an intro to the trade desk contact — frame it as helping them route it correctly, not going around anyone
→ Find out if there's a PMP path — trade desks love PMPs because it still looks like programmatic to them
→ Offer to write the one-pager the planner can share internally — do the work for them
Price "You're too expensive." ›
Why reps lose here
They immediately defend the price or start discounting. Both moves signal the price wasn't justified. "Too expensive" almost always means "I don't see the value yet" — not "the number is too high."
What to say
Word for word →"Compared to what? I want to make sure we're comparing the right things. If you're looking at a lower CPM somewhere else, what does their audience look like against yours? What are their viewability and completion rates? Because on a cost-per-outcome basis, we usually look very different."
Next moves
→ Never discount first — ask what budget they do have and build a package around it
→ Reframe from CPM to cost-per-outcome — viewability, completed views, site visits, whatever their KPI is
→ Ask what a successful campaign looks like — anchor on results, not rate
Approval "The client didn't approve you." ›
Why reps lose here
Reps accept this and disappear. But "the client didn't approve" usually means the agency didn't advocate for you — because they didn't have enough to make the case. Clients rarely have strong opinions about specific publishers unless someone put one in front of them.
What to say
Word for word →"Helpful to know — can I ask, was it that the client specifically said no to us, or that we weren't on the approved list they were working from? Those are pretty different situations. If it's the latter, I'd love to help you build the case to get us added. I can put something together specifically for their audience and goals."
Next moves
→ Offer to build a custom one-pager the agency can send to the brand — make it easy for them to advocate for you
→ Ask about the path to get on the approved vendor list for future campaigns
→ Find out who at the brand actually controls vendor approvals — often it's media manager, legal, or procurement
